Crisp

Gyoza

Prep 35 mins / Cook 12 mins / Makes 24

Good gyoza is all contrast: a lacy crisp base, a soft wrapper, and a juicy filling. The key is salting the cabbage first so the dumplings stay tender instead of watery.

Ingredients

  • 24 gyoza wrappers
  • 250 g ground pork
  • 2 cups finely shredded cabbage
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 garlic clove, grated
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Neutral oil and 1/3 cup water, for cooking

Method

  1. Toss the cabbage with a pinch of salt. Rest for 10 minutes, then squeeze out as much liquid as possible.
  2. Mix cabbage, pork, green onion, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, and sesame oil until sticky.
  3. Place a teaspoon of filling in each wrapper, wet the edge, fold, and pleat closed.
  4. Heat oil in a nonstick pan. Add gyoza flat-side down and cook until the bottoms brown.
  5. Add water, cover, and steam for 5 minutes. Uncover and cook until the bottoms crisp again.

Finish

Serve with equal parts soy sauce and rice vinegar, plus a few drops of chili oil if you want heat.
