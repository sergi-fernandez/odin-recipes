Shoyu Ramen

Prep 10 mins / Cook 25 mins / Serves 2

This is a weeknight ramen built around a clean soy broth. It is not trying to be a long-simmered shop bowl; it is fast, aromatic, and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 2 portions fresh or dried ramen noodles
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 4 thin slices fresh ginger
  • 2 soft-boiled eggs
  • Green onions, spinach, mushrooms, or nori for topping

Method

  1. Simmer stock with garlic and ginger for 15 minutes, then remove the aromatics.
  2. Stir in soy sauce, mirin, and sesame oil. Taste and adjust with more soy if needed.
  3. Cook noodles separately until just tender, then drain.
  4. Divide noodles between bowls and ladle hot broth over them.
  5. Top with halved eggs, green onions, and any vegetables you are using.

Finish

Add toppings at the end so they stay distinct. A small spoon of miso or chili crisp makes the broth richer.
